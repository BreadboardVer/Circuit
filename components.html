USER'S GUIDELINES

This user's manual hopes to help users better understand how to use this virtual circuit board for verification and debugging purposes

Building components onto the breadboard

(Note: The following steps are applicable to all the components except the 'wire' and 'volt src')

Log in to your student account which would bring you to the breadboard page
Suppose you want to put resistor between hole A2 and hole A5,
Step (1) : Click onto the 'Resistor' icon located under Core Parts section
Step (2) : Click the hole on the breadboard located at position A2
Step (3) : click 'Done' button located under Core Parts
Resistor appears with left node at A2 and right node at A5 shown in first image above
The same applies if you desire to put the opamp
Repeat steps (1)-(3) with starting hole now at E2
You will see opamp appears ash shown in the second image above

Steps to put wire and volt src onto the breadboard

Step (1) : Select the resistor icon
Step (2) : Select the starting hole (let's say postion A1)
Step (3) : Select the end hole (suppose position C6)
Step (4) : Click 'Done' button
As shown above, wire stretched from hole A1 and ends at C6

Selecting colours for the components

(Note: This is only applicable for resistor and wire)

Step (1) : Select the resistor icon
Step (2) : Under Inspector section, select desired band colours

(refer to the above image)

Step (3) : Select the hole to place the beginning node of resistor
Step (4) : Click 'Done' button
Repeat Steps (1)-(4) for wire to choose desired wire colour

Delete function

Step (1) : Select the component that you desire to delete
Step (2) : Click 'Delete' icon

How to save your Circuit as PNG

Step (1) : Construct your desired circuit
Step (2) : When want to download it as SVG, click 'Download PNG' icon
Step (3) : Downloaded SVG file located in your PC Downloads

How to save your Circuit for future editing purposes

Step (1) : Construct your desired circuit
Step (2) : Click 'Download Json' icon
Step (3) : Now, you can find your downloaded Json in your PC Downloads
(Note: You are strongly encouraged to rename your file according to your preference so as to avoid confusion when recalling them whenever you desire to edit it again)

How to recall your saved Circuit for editing purposes

Step (1) : Click 'Choose file'
Step (2) : Select the desired saved circuit that you wish to edit
Step (3) : Then, click 'Continue' button located at the footer tab and your circuit would appear.
Note: You may now continue to edit by removing the existing components or adding new components.

Steps to verify your circuit against prof's circuit and reading error messages

Step (1) : Make sure your circuit is present on the virtual breadboard
Step (2) : Next, click 'Choose file'
Step (2) : Select the professor's model circuit that you have downloaded from school learning platform (Luminus)
Step (3) : Then, click 'Verify' button located at the footer tab
[Note: If your circuit is correctly constructed, no error message would show. However, if your circuit is different from model circuit, error messages would appear at 'Verification' section on the bottom right hand corner. Furthermore, wrong component or redundant component would be highlighted on the breadboard. You could also check out the error messages by hovering over those highlighted components.]
